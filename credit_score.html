Introduction

A credit scoring system is a crucial tool used by banks and financial institutions to assess the creditworthiness of individuals applying for loans or credit. The system assigns a credit score to each applicant based on various financial and personal factors, helping lenders make informed decisions about lending risk.

This module helps in evaluating a transaction or customer's credibility to perform further actions such as issuing a credit card or giving a balance transfer offer for high-credit customers in a credit card company The process involves several steps, including data preprocessing, feature filtering, feature binning, transforming to Weight of Evidence (WOE), model tuning, scorecard generation, distribution analysis, and threshold tuning.

Architecture




This module provides a foundational structure for building a credit scoring system, which involves data preprocessing, feature engineering, model training, and scorecard generation.
